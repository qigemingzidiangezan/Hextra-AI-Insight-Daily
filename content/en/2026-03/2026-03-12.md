### Cutting-Edge Research

1. **ColQwen3.5-v1 has topped the document retrieval leaderboard.**
**ColQwen3.5-v1**, a [retrieval model based on Qwen3.5 (AI News)](https://huggingface.co/athrael-soju/colqwen3.5-v1), has been released by developers. Despite having only **4.5B** parameters, it **dominated** the ViDoRe leaderboard, achieving an astonishing **nDCG@5** score of **0.917**. 🏆 This model is [deeply optimized (AI News)](https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/comments/1rqvv3z/p_colqwen35v1_45b_sota_on_vidore_v1_ndcg5_0917/) for complex documents like financial tables, making it currently the strongest open-source solution in the retrieval domain.

2. **The IDP Multimodal Document AI Leaderboard has been released.**
A brand-new [document intelligence evaluation standard (AI News)](http://idp-leaderboard.org) has officially dropped. 📊 It covers **16 visual language models** and over **9000 documents**. **Gemini 3.1** is currently at the top of the leaderboard, showing impressive performance. However, handwritten OCR and complex tables are still major industry bottlenecks. The complete dataset is now [open for download on HuggingFace (AI News)](https://nanonets.com/blog/idp-leaderboard-1-5/).

3. **Structured Prompting has successfully tackled the challenge of rare language translation.**
A new study has proposed a solution for [extremely low-resource language translation (AI News)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.15378v1). This method can reduce **lexical contamination** from 80% to just **5%** without any fine-tuning. The approach, based on **structured prompting**, is both simple and highly efficient. Its feasibility was [verified using Tulu as a case study (AI News)](https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/comments/1rqxr78/p_structured_prompting_for_extremely_lowresource/), offering a fresh perspective for endangered language preservation. 🌱

His world model company, **AMI**, just closed a [seed round of $1.03 billion (AI News)](https://www.qbitai.com/2026/03/386308.html), with **Xie Saining** joining the team as Chief Scientist.
